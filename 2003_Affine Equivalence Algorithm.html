Affine Equivalence

Two functions F:{0,1}n→{0,1}m\bm{F}:\left\{0,1\right\}^{n}\rightarrow \left\{0,1\right\}^{m}F:{0,1}n→{0,1}m, G:{0,1}n→{0,1}m\bm{G}:\left\{0,1\right\}^{n}\rightarrow \left\{0,1\right\}^{m}G:{0,1}n→{0,1}m are affine equivalent is there exist two invertible affine transformations A1:{0,1}n→{0,1}nA_{1}:\left\{0,1\right\}^{n}\rightarrow \left\{0,1\right\}^{n}A1​:{0,1}n→{0,1}n and A2:{0,1}m→{0,1}mA_{2}:\left\{0,1\right\}^{m}\rightarrow \left\{0,1\right\}^{m}A2​:{0,1}m→{0,1}m such that

G=A2∘F∘A1\bm{G} = A_{2}\circ \bm{F} \circ A_{1}G=A2​∘F∘A1​

It is easy to show that the affine equivalence relation partitions the set of all functions into (affine) equivalence classes. We denote

F≡G\bm{F} \equiv \bm{G}F≡G

if F\bm{F}F is affine equivalent to G\bm{G}G.

Problem

Problem 1. Linear equivalence

By linear mapping we mean a mapping L(x)L(x)L(x) over GF(2)nGF(2)^{n}GF(2)n that satisfies

L(x+y)=L(x)+L(y)L(x+y)=L(x)+L(y)L(x+y)=L(x)+L(y)

Let us consider the prblem of checking linear equivalence between two S-boxes S1S_{1}S1​ and S2S_{2}S2​. The problem is to find two invertible linear mappings L1L_{1}L1​ and L2L_{2}L2​, such that

L2∘S1∘L1=S2L_{2} \circ S_{1} \circ L_{1} =S_{2}L2​∘S1​∘L1​=S2​

Problem 2. Affine equivalence

We want an algorithm that takes two n×nn \times nn×n-bit S-boxes S1S_{1}S1​ and S2S_{2}S2​ as input, and checks whether there exists a pair of invertible affine mappings A1A_{1}A1​ and A2A_{2}A2​ such that

A2∘S1∘A1=S2A_{2}\circ S_{1}\circ A_{1}=S_{2}A2​∘S1​∘A1​=S2​

The Linear Equivalence Algorithm 1

Naive approach

Guess one of the mappings, for example L1L_{1}L1​. Then one can extract L2L_{2}L2​ from the equation:

L2=S2∘L1−1∘S1−1L_{2} = S_{2} \circ L_{1}^{-1} \circ S_{1}^{-1}L2​=S2​∘L1−1​∘S1−1​

and check if it is a linear, invertible mapping.

There are O(2n2)O(2^{n^{2}})O(2n2) choices of invertible linear mappings over nnn-bit vectors.
For each guess one will need about n3n^{3}n3 steps to check for linearity and invertibility using Gaussian elimination [1].

In total the naive algorithm would require O(n32n2)O(n^{3}2^{n^{2}})O(n32n2) steps.

A similar naive affine equivalence algorrithm will use O(n32n(n+1))O(n^{3}2^{n (n+1)})O(n32n(n+1)).

Improved Naive approach

We need only nnn equations in order to check L2L_{2}L2​ for invertibility and linearity.
If one guesses only log⁡2n\log_{2}nlog2​n vectors from L1L_{1}L1​ one may span a space of nnn points (by trying all linear combinations of the guessed vectors), evaluate the results through L1L_{1}L1​, S1S_{1}S1​ and S2S_{2}S2​ and have nnn constraints required to check for linearity of L2L_{2}L2​.
If the nnn new equations are not independent one will need to guess additional vectors of L1L_{1}L1​.

Such an algorithm would require guessing of nlog⁡2nn\log_2nnlog2​n bits of L1L_{1}L1​ and the total complexity would be O(n32nlog⁡n)O(n^{3}2^{n\log n})O(n32nlogn)

Exploit two ideas

  1. needlework effect: in which guesses of portions from L1L_{1}L1​ provide us with free knowledge of the values of L2L_{2}L2​.
  2. exponential amplification of guesses: due to the linear (affine) structure of the mappings, support that the new values from L2L_{2}L2​ allow us to extract new free information about L1L_{1}L1​.
  3. knowing kkk vectors from the mapping L1L_{1}L1​, we know 2k2^k2k linear combinations of these vectors for free.

Finding the linear representative

The efficiency of an algorithm that finds the linear representative RSR_SRS​ for an S-box SSS depends on how this unique representative is chosen.

Defination of linear representative

If all S-boxes in a linear equivalence class are ordered lexicographically according to their lookup tables, then the smallest is called the representative of the class.

Construst the representative RSR_SRS​

  1. Making an initial guess.

  2. Incrementally build the linear mappings AAA and BBB such that RS′=B−1∘S∘AR'_{S}=B^{-1} \circ S \circ ARS′​=B−1∘S∘A is as small as possible.

  3. The representative RSR_SRS​ is obtained by taking the smallest RS′R'_{S}RS′​ over possible guesses.

Complexity of AE

Completely defining RS′R'_SRS′​ for a particular guess takes about 2n2^n2n steps.
However, most guesses will already be rejected after having determined only slightly more than nnn values, because at that point RS′R'_SRS′​ will usually turn out to be larger than the current smallest candidate.
The total complexity of finding the representative is expected to be O(n32n)O(n^32^n)O(n32n).
The affine equivalence algorithm (AE) has complexity O(n322n)O(n^32^{2n})O(n322n).
